Optional Module: Machine Learning and “AI”

This module is intended to prompt you to think about “AI” and machine learning in DH and in the digital world more generally. Note that I’m putting “AI” in scare quotes. That’s because what techbros and the media are breathlessly hyping as “AI” is not what people used to define AI as even 5 years ago and is not particularly intelligent, either. It is an extension of existing machine learning techniques (especially large language models) that has undergone rapid development and popularization in the last few years. As such, most of the readings are short and very new!

10. write a blog post or participate in the Slack channel for this module on the discussion on machine learning, large language modules, and “AI.” In what ways can new technologies be usefully transformative for society? For the digital humanities? For history in particular? And in what ways do they open up new avenues for abuse? Are those avenues completely new vs. building upon problems already extant in society? How should copyright and ethics play into this? What role do humanists have to play in conceptualizing and implementing new uses of AI?

Technical Activities

1. pick an “AI” platform like ChatGPT, GitHub CoPilot, DeepAI, Midjourney, etc. and experiment with it. What are the affordances of the platform – that is, what does it let you do easily and what doesn’t it let you do? In what ways can this potentially be helpful for people? In what ways can it potentially be harmful?
