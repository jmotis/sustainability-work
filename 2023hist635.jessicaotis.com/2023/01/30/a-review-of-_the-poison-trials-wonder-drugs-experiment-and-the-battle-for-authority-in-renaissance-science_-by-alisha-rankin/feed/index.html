One of the first things that struck me was the connection between experimentation and early modern medicine. I was left wondering why so much of early modern medicine was based on leeches and bloodletting. Were people set in their ways about the traditional remedies? Or could the nobles afford to be treated with a new “wonder drug”, while leaving the ordinary people with the traditional remedies? As regards medical ethics, I was surprised to hear that the Renaissance doctors knew that the poison trials were ethically questionable at best. Why didn’t they stop? However, my question was answered when I learned that the first code of medical ethics was published in 1803. The Age of Enlightenment had to dawn before people questioned dubious medical practices, and the medical ethics we know today came in the wake of World War II, which the people of the sixteenth century could never have foreseen.
