]]> By: Alexa Shipes https://dev.jessicaotis.com/2023/02/13/the-astronomer-the-witch-a-review/#comment-17 Tue, 14 Feb 2023 16:53:05 +0000 https://dev.jessicaotis.com/?p=531#comment-17 I really enjoyed this review and the book. Your point about astrology as an “early form of psychoanalysis” made me think of how sixteenth-century people looked to dreams in order to interpret the subconscious mind. Hundreds of years later, Sigmund Freud would state that dreams were a path to interpreting the unconscious mind. I enjoyed this book because it gave me a perspective on why there were so many witch trials in early seventeenth-century Germany. I also enjoyed the fact that Kepler wrote a science fiction novel centuries before H.G. Wells wrote War of the Worlds. Your point about the book humanizing Kepler resonated with me. In high school, we’re taught about all of these big names in science, but the teachers never delve into who they were or what motivated them. To many people, the big names in science are just that: big names with dates in long-past centuries attached to them. The book doesn’t just humanize Kepler, though; it brings a human face to the witch trials in early modern Germany.
